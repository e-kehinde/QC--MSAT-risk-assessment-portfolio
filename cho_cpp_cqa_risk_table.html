# CPP Linked CQAs Mechanism of impact
1 Dissolved oxygen (DO) Glycosylation Potency DO drives nucleotide sugar availability for N-glycan synthesis. Hypoxia (<20% sat.) depletes UDP-galactose and UDP-GlcNAc, reducing galactosylation and sialylation. Low DO also compromises cell energetics and productivity, affecting titre and product-related variants.
2 pH Glycosylation Charge variants Aggregation / fragments pH affects intracellular Golgi pH, which is critical for glycosyltransferase activity — shifts outside 6.8–7.2 alter galactosylation and sialylation profiles. Acidic excursions drive aspartate isomerisation and deamidation (Asn→Asp/isoAsp), altering the charge variant profile. Alkaline conditions promote disulfide scrambling and aggregation.
3 Temperature Glycosylation Charge variants Aggregation / fragments Potency Temperature shifts (e.g. 37→33°C) modulate cell-specific productivity and glycan processing kinetics. Lower temperatures slow sialylation enzyme turnover, yielding lower sialic acid content but often higher galactosylation. Elevated temperatures increase proteolytic activity (fragmenting the mAb) and non-enzymatic modifications (deamidation). Temperature-shift timing is a key process lever in fed-batch design.
4 Agitation rate / shear Aggregation / fragments Purity / process-related impurities Excessive impeller speed or sparger bubble burst energy causes hydrodynamic shear stress, lysing cells and releasing host-cell proteins, DNA, and proteases. Sublethal shear alters cell membrane integrity without lysis, increasing HCP secretion. Localised cavitation near spargers is a key shear hotspot in large-scale vessels. Also promotes mAb aggregate formation at gas–liquid interfaces.
5 Osmolality Glycosylation Charge variants Purity / process-related impurities Hyperosmolar conditions (>450 mOsm/kg) — common with concentrated feed addition or excess NaHCO₃ correction — decrease cell growth and alter nucleotide sugar metabolism, shifting glycoforms. Osmotic stress induces unfolded protein response, increasing misfolded species and clipped variants. Elevated NaCl specifically impacts sialylation through CMP-sialic acid transporter competition.
6 Glucose / lactate levels Glycosylation Potency Purity / process-related impurities Glucose is the primary carbon source for nucleotide sugar (UDP-GlcNAc, GDP-mannose) biosynthesis feeding the hexosamine pathway. Glucose limitation reduces Man5 occupancy and truncates glycan processing. Lactate accumulation (acidosis) further suppresses glycosylation enzyme activity and raises osmolality via counterion correction. Lactate shifts above ~15 mM are critical risk points in late fed-batch.
7 Feed rate / feeding strategy Glycosylation Charge variants HCP / DNA Feed composition and timing directly controls intracellular amino acid and nucleotide sugar pools. Glutamine limitation promotes asparagine deamidation (charge variant impact). Manganese supplementation in feed strongly drives galactosylation and sialylation via β-1,4-galactosyltransferase activation. Overfeeding drives lactate accumulation; underfeeding limits cell viability and increases apoptotic debris (HCP, DNA).
8 pCO₂ Glycosylation Charge variants Aggregation / fragments Elevated pCO₂ (>150 mmHg) acidifies intracellular pH independent of bulk pH control, suppressing Golgi glycosyltransferase activity and reducing sialylation. High CO₂ also promotes lysine carbamylation, creating acidic charge variants. In large-scale vessels, CO₂ stripping is mass-transfer limited, making pCO₂ a critical scale-up parameter. Chronic high pCO₂ impairs cell growth and induces stress responses.
9 Cell density / seeding density Glycosylation HCP / DNA Purity / process-related impurities Seeding density affects the growth phase trajectory and time-in-culture, which controls the residence time of product in the bioreactor under potential enzymatic degradation. High peak VCD generates more metabolic waste and increases culture heterogeneity. Late-culture viability decline drives HCP and DNA release from lysed cells. Consistent N-1 seeding density is a critical upstream control to ensure reproducible glycosylation and impurity profiles.
10 Culture duration / harvest timing Charge variants Aggregation / fragments HCP / DNA Purity / process-related impurities Extended culture duration increases product exposure to secreted proteases (fragments), reduces viability below thresholds (typically <60%), and elevates HCP/DNA burden from cell lysis. Time-dependent non-enzymatic modifications (deamidation, isomerisation, oxidation) accumulate as acidic and oxidised charge variants. Harvest timing is typically controlled by a combination of time, VCD, and viability criteria to balance titre vs. quality.
